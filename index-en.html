What is Hepatitis B?

Hepatitis B is the most widespread serious liver infection in the world. It is caused by the hepatitis B virus that attacks and damages the liver. Every year, up to one million people die from hepatitis B, although it is preventable and treatable.

Hepatitis B is transmitted through direct contact with infected blood or certain body fluids. The virus is most often transmitted from an infected pregnant woman to her baby during childbirth, due to blood exchange between mother and baby.

Hepatitis B is a "silent epidemic" because most recently or chronically infected people show no symptoms. They can therefore transmit the virus to others without knowing it and perpetuate the silent spread of hepatitis B.

The good news is that hepatitis B is preventable and treatable. A simple blood test can diagnose hepatitis B virus infection. Testing is the only way to know for sure if you're infected.
